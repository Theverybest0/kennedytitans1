Our Story

About

Who We Are

Built from
Passion, Driven
by Purpose

Science Titans began as the Science Warriors — a Division B team at G.M. Walters Middle School in Fremont, California. What started as a group of curious, competitive students has grown into one of the most driven Science Olympiad programs in the region.

Now competing as Science Titans in Division C at Kennedy High School, our team has carried every lesson, every late-night study session, and every hard-earned medal from our middle school years into this next chapter. We placed 5th at Regionals and 11th at States — and we're just getting started.

We are students, scientists, builders, and problem-solvers — currently in 8th grade and heading into Division C as 9th graders. Our competitive drive is matched only by our commitment to each other and to the science we love.

Our History

How We Got Here

The Origin — G.M. Walters Middle School
Science Warriors, Division B
The team formed at G.M. Walters Middle School in Fremont, California, competing under the name Science Warriors in Division B. A core group of passionate, hard-working students built the foundation of what would become Science Titans.
Growth — Medals & Milestones
First Medals on the Board
Through relentless preparation and teamwork, the Science Warriors began earning medals — including gold and silver finishes — establishing ourselves as a competitive force in the Division B circuit.
The Transition — Kennedy High School
Science Titans, Division C
Moving up to Division C at Kennedy High School, we rebranded as the Science Titans — the same team, same spirit, bigger stage. Our Division B experience gave us a powerful competitive edge heading into high school.
Now — 2025 Season
5th Regionals · 11th States
In our Division C debut season, we placed 5th at Regionals and 11th at States — a historic result for a first-year high school team. With 15 members and a full 23-event roster, the best is still ahead.

Core Values

The Principles
That Guide Us

🔭 Scientific Excellence

We hold ourselves to the highest academic standard. Our preparation is thorough, our testing is rigorous, and our commitment to getting science right is non-negotiable.

🤲 Inclusive Collaboration

Every voice matters. We build teams where all abilities, backgrounds, and learning styles are respected and celebrated — because the best science happens when diverse minds work together.

🌱 Growth Mindset

We embrace failure as data and mistakes as teachers. Our culture prizes learning over performance, and improvement over perfection — always pushing toward what's possible.

🏛️ Community Responsibility

Science is a public good. We take responsibility for sharing knowledge and inspiring scientific curiosity — in our school, our community, and beyond.

Strategic Goals

What We're
Working Toward

01
Compete at the State Level

Our primary competitive goal is to build on our 5th-place regional finish and advance deeper into the State Tournament, where we aim to crack the top 5 statewide.

02
Achieve Full Event Coverage

We aim to field competitive, prepared participants in all 23 Science Olympiad events — ensuring no event is forfeited and every member is trained and tested.

03
Build a Sustainable Program

Through mentorship, documentation, and alumni networks, we're building a program that outlasts any individual season — a legacy that grows stronger each year.

04
Expand STEM Outreach

We commit to hosting at minimum two outreach events per year targeting middle school students — introducing them to Science Olympiad and igniting a passion for STEM early.

05
Secure Institutional Funding

We're actively pursuing sponsorships and grants to fully fund competition registration, travel, lab materials, and equipment — making participation accessible to all members regardless of financial background.
